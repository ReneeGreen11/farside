I have been hard at work learning a little bit about how to make a website. I have made my own website and moved my domain away from Substack. *Why?*

There are many reasons to do this, here are some of my reasons:

{{< audio src="/audio/Farside_moved.mp3" caption="Listen to this track" >}}

1. I live in Canada, where there are currently efforts to introduce age verification for the use of social media. Substack is social media. What this means is that access to these platforms may become increasingly restricted, with people required to complete face scans or provide identification to use them. Perhaps many will comply—I’m not sure. I hope they won’t. I also hope people begin stepping away from these platforms before such measures are even implemented.

Social media has not been favourable to individual freedom, and it seems to have worsened since COVID. I am convinced that many infringements on freedom, including those during COVID, would not have been possible without the influence and reach of social media.

2. It is a fake place to be. I have subscribers here who never spend a moment looking at my blog. I suppose they think they are doing me a favour by subscribing, but it is not helpful to have subscribers who do not actually engage with the content. I have written about this before. Additionally, programmers and AI appear to play a role in boosting some accounts while stifling others.

Since publishing my new site I have been using Google for a lot of questions about how to get things done. Recently I asked Google to find my site, and it did a short write up about my blog. Here I will let you in on the conversation that happened and my thoughts about that conversation. Which pertain to what is really going on on the internet. It is quite an illusion in my view.

Google: Far Side of Fringe is a reader-supported Canadian newsletter written by Renee Green, hosted on Substack 

About - Far Side of Fringe. 
The publication features a mix of commentary, memes, and opinion pieces covering:

Political & Social Commentary: Letters and critiques focused on democratic standards in Canada.
Cultural & Health Memes: Lighthearted and satirical posts regarding various news events and viral topics Bird Flu, Flirt Flu MEMES - by Renee Green - Far Side of Fringe.

To read the publication, explore past editions, or manage your subscription, you can visit the Far Side of Fringe Sitemap.

My thoughts: it is interesting to me that what Google notes about my substack is everything except for how THERE WAS NO PANDEMIC. Is AI restricted from seeing that about my blog? It would make sense that if you wanted to censor this view, AI would need to be restricted from seeing it as well. It saw that I have a lot of memes posts, which I have since stopped doing. And the reason I stopped doing them is that I became wary of the possibility that memes posts were so popular because they were allowed. You are allowed to do memes posts because they do not truly threaten this new system being put in place around us. Entertainment is encouraged because they want people to stay idle and not become disruptive. Of course, they'd rather we did not do memes either, but if there is something that they must live with. They will live with memes.
